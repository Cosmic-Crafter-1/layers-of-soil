Humus Layer

The top layer of soil, rich in organic matter.

Characteristics:

  • Contains decomposed plant and animal material
  • Rich in nutrients for plant growth
  • Dark in color due to high organic content
  • Home to many microorganisms and earthworms

Topsoil

The upper layer of soil where most plant roots grow.

Characteristics:

  • Contains minerals, organic matter, air, and water
  • Most biologically active soil layer
  • Typically extends 5-10 inches below surface
  • Crucial for agriculture and plant growth

Subsoil

Layer beneath topsoil, with less organic matter.

Characteristics:

  • Contains more clay and minerals than topsoil
  • Stores water and nutrients for deep plant roots
  • Often lighter in color than topsoil
  • Contains weathered parent material

Parent Rock

Partially weathered bedrock transitioning to soil.

Characteristics:

  • Source of mineral particles for the soil above
  • Consists of weathered fragments of bedrock
  • Influences soil chemistry and properties
  • Limited biological activity

Bedrock

Solid rock layer beneath all soil layers.

Characteristics:

  • Forms the foundation of soil formation
  • Can be igneous, sedimentary, or metamorphic rock
  • Largely unweathered and solid
  • Very slow to change over geological time
